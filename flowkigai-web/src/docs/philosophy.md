# Flowkigai — Philosophy & Guide

![A still mountain lake at dawn — calm, open, full of possibility](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1506905925346-21bda4d32df4?w=760&h=340&q=80&auto=format&fit=crop)

## The Problem We're Solving

Most productivity tools fail in one of two ways.

**Too tactical:** They give you lists, timers, and dashboards — but no answer to *why* any of it matters. You're efficient at climbing the ladder, but you never asked whether it's leaning against the right wall.

**Too abstract:** They ask you to "discover your purpose" with inspiring quotes and vision boards, but offer no mechanism to turn insight into sustained daily action.

Flowkigai bridges that gap. It starts with the deepest question — *what gives your life meaning?* — and then provides a practical system to pursue it with focus, energy, and rhythm.

## What is Ikigai?

Ikigai (生き甲斐) is a Japanese concept meaning *reason for being* — the sweet spot where four life dimensions overlap:

- **What you love** — activities that make you lose track of time
- **What you're good at** — your natural talents and developed skills
- **What the world needs** — problems you can help solve
- **What you can be paid for** — the economic reality of sustainable living

The Flowkigai Ikigai Journey takes you through four dedicated rooms — one per dimension — using reflective prompts to surface your authentic answers. Once you've explored all four, the **North Star** synthesis page helps you craft a single unifying statement that anchors every goal and project you undertake.

> The goal isn't a perfect philosophical answer. It's a working hypothesis that guides you — one you revisit and refine each year.

## What is Flow?

![Deep focus — a person absorbed in creative work, the world around them quiet](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1499750310107-5fef28a66643?w=760&h=300&q=80&auto=format&fit=crop)

Flow is a state of optimal experience described by psychologist Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi after decades of research. When in flow, you are:

- Completely absorbed in the task
- Losing track of time
- Intrinsically motivated — no external reward needed
- Performing at or near your peak

**The key mechanism: challenge/skill balance.** Flow lives in the channel between boredom (skill exceeds challenge) and anxiety (challenge exceeds skill). It requires tasks that stretch you — but not so far that you freeze.

Csikszentmihalyi identified **8 conditions** for flow:

1. Clear goals at each step
2. Immediate feedback on progress
3. Balance between challenge and skill
4. Merging of action and awareness
5. Elimination of distractions
6. No fear of failure (loss of self-consciousness)
7. Time distortion (hours feel like minutes)
8. The activity is autotelic — rewarding in itself

**How Flowkigai operationalises this:**

Before each session you set an *energy level*, *session type* (Deep Work, Learning, Creative, Administrative), and a *specific intention*. The timer runs in focused blocks with a micro-review pause at the end. The micro-review captures: what was accomplished, how focused you were, and what would make the next session better. This feedback loop is the engine.

## What are SMART Goals?

SMART is a framework for writing goals that are actually achievable:

| Letter | Meaning | Poor example | SMART example |
|--------|---------|--------------|---------------|
| **S** — Specific | Precise, not vague | "Get fit" | "Run 5 km without stopping" |
| **M** — Measurable | You can track progress | "Read more" | "Read 2 books per month" |
| **A** — Achievable | Challenging but realistic | "Write a novel in a week" | "Write 500 words per day" |
| **R** — Relevant | Aligned with your North Star | "Learn calligraphy" (random) | "Learn Spanish for the Barcelona sabbatical" |
| **T** — Time-bound | Has a deadline | "Someday improve my diet" | "Follow a Mediterranean diet for 90 days" |

In Flowkigai, every goal is linked to your Ikigai North Star. The **Relevant** criterion is therefore automatic — if a goal doesn't connect to your purpose, it shouldn't be in the system.

## Goal Types

Flowkigai recognises three types of goals, each with different tracking mechanics:

**Deep Work** — A project requiring extended concentration over weeks or months. Examples: writing a book, learning a skill, building a product. *Maximum 3 active Deep Work goals at a time* (Essentialism principle: if everything is a priority, nothing is).

**Habit** — A behaviour you want to repeat consistently, tracked by streak. Examples: daily meditation, weekly exercise, morning journaling. Identity-based in the Atomic Habits sense: you're not "trying to exercise" — you're *becoming* someone who exercises.

**Milestone** — A one-time achievement with a clear done/not-done binary. Examples: pass an exam, launch a feature, run a half-marathon.

## The Weekly Review

Borrowed from David Allen's GTD system, the Weekly Review is a non-negotiable ritual — the glue between daily execution and long-term direction. It has three sections:

1. **Reflect** — What went well? What drained you? What surprised you?
2. **Review** — Check each goal and habit. Are you on track? What's blocking you?
3. **Plan** — Set your top 3 priorities for next week. What one thing, if done, would make everything else easier?

Each section auto-saves as you type. The review is scoped to an ISO week — one per week, always revisable until the next one begins.

## How It All Connects

![A night sky full of stars — every one a fixed point to navigate by](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1511632765486-a01980e01a18?w=760&h=300&q=80&auto=format&fit=crop)

The full loop, from discovery to daily action:

```
Ikigai Journey (4 rooms)
        ↓
   North Star (your purpose in one sentence)
        ↓
   Mind Map (brainstorm everything you want)
        ↓
   Goals (3 types, SMART, linked to North Star)
        ↓
   Tasks (Next Actions per goal, GTD-style)
        ↓
   Flow Sessions (deep work blocks with micro-reviews)
        ↓
   Weekly Review (reflect → review → plan)
        ↓
   Dashboard (see the pattern, course-correct)
        ↓
   (next year: revisit Ikigai, update North Star)
```

This loop is designed so that every task you do in a day traces back to a goal, every goal traces back to your North Star, and your North Star traces back to your deepest values. **Alignment is not a metaphor here — it's a structural property of the system.**

When something doesn't fit the loop, Flowkigai makes that visible. That friction is information.

## A Note on Pace

Flowkigai is not a hustle tool. It is a *clarity* tool.

The constraint of 3 Deep Work goals isn't a limitation — it's a decision. The Weekly Review isn't a chore — it's a signal that you're taking your time seriously. The Ikigai Journey isn't a one-time exercise — it's an annual calibration.

The goal is a life where the things you spend your hours on are the things that matter most to you. That's harder to achieve than it sounds, and it requires a system that works with your nature rather than against it.

That's what Flowkigai is for.
